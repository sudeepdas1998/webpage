Welcome

I am a Project Associate at the Ocean Analysis and Modeling Laboratory, School of Earth, Ocean, and Climate Sciences, IIT Bhubaneswar. My research examines the influence of oceanic processes on biophysical parameters—such as phytoplankton dynamics and dissolved oxygen—in the Bay of Bengal. In the long term, I aim to understand the impacts of climate change on ocean productivity.

I specialize in working with diverse datasets and methods, including remote sensing, advanced statistics, modeling, in-situ observations, and fieldwork. Originally from Bhubaneswar, the coastal heritage "Temple City," I draw inspiration from both science and place in my work. I have submitted my PhD thesis for review in August 2025.

I am open to discussions and collaborations. You can find more about me and my research through the quick links to my CV, research portfolio, and resume page provided below.

Research

Current Research (Dissolved Oxygen in Bay of Bengal)

Oxygen Minimum Zones (OMZs) in the Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal are key to marine biogeochemical cycles, particularly nitrogen cycling and greenhouse gas production. In the Bay of Bengal, deep-water transport plays a critical role in sustaining oxygen levels, with circulation at depths >1000 m differing from surface dynamics. To investigate these processes, I use the CROCO-PISCES coupled circulation–biogeochemical model to simulate deep dissolved oxygen (DDO) and assess the influence of deep-water oxygen supply on long-term oxygen dynamics.
